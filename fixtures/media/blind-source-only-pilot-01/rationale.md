# Creative rationale

The two sources are landscape, floor-level, locked-camera takes of the same solo studio choreography. The performer wears the same white top, cap, loose gray pants and sneakers in both takes, and the movement vocabulary repeats: compact torso isolations, hand-led shapes, a leg lift, long arm sweeps, a grounded wide stance and a late high-knee reach. That repetition supports a simple creator-reel premise: **two takes, one pocket**.

The edit is intentionally nonchronological. It opens on one compact impact from each take, then backs into the smaller groove and grows toward the widest shapes. The middle and peak lean on source A because its background stays clean through the strongest wide-stance phrase. Source B is used early, immediately before the background passerby enters, and again late after the background clears. Camera setup, long waiting beats and the walk back to the lens are omitted.

The landscape originals cannot survive a literal 9:16 center crop without losing much of the arm choreography. The chosen treatment places a sharp 4:5-ish crop over a dark, blurred full-height duplicate. This keeps the dancer large while preserving more lateral gesture. Only the two opening hits use a tighter vertical crop; the visual expansion into the wider stage becomes part of the energy arc.

Text is deliberately sparse. “TWO TAKES” states the visible premise, “ONE POCKET” names the rhythmic consistency across the performances, and “LET IT LAND” cues the final high-knee/open-arm phrase. The custom block lettering, dark banner and lime rule stay above the dancer and away from the usual Instagram control area.

Both sources contain continuous, unidentified rehearsal playback rather than useful dialogue. The measured integrated levels were approximately -21.1 LUFS for source A and -20.5 LUFS for source B, with no meaningful silence runs at the tested threshold. Because that playback is not rights-cleared, all camera audio is muted. The preview uses only a generated 108 BPM metronome. Source-B onset analysis and the visible spacing of the dance accents support a 106–110 BPM handoff range, with a bass-forward, syncopated pop/R&B or Afro-pop groove as the best fit.

No target edit, target-derived asset, prior plan or reference audio informed these choices. The visual story, crop strategy, pacing, text and music archetype come from the two permitted source takes; public catalog metadata was used only to freeze one optional Instagram search candidate, whose Instagram availability remains unverified.
